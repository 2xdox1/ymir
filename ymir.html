Ymir, the ancestor of all jötnar.
The father of the race of giants in Norse mythology and the grandfather of Odin, King of the Gods.
What do we know about Ymir?
Ymir is the primordial giant existing before the creation of the world.
He was formed from the meeting of ice and heat in Ginnungagap.
He is the ancestor of the frost giants in Norse mythology.
Ymir was nourished by the milk of the primeval cow Audumbla.
Of old was the age when Ymir lived;
Sea nor cool waves nor sand there were;
Earth had not been, nor heaven above,
But a yawning gap, and grass nowhere.
- Poetic Edda, Völuspá
 (H.A. Bellows translation)
